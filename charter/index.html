Charter for the Formation of an Infaruan State


Article 1. The Constituent Assembly

The Constituent Assembly of Infaru is made up of delegates sent by groups of 5 players submitted to the Interim Administration, these delegates may be recalled and replaced by a majority of the players that sent them. Delegates are tasked with establishing a constitution and Infaruan State. The Interim Administration may take an advisory role in the process of creating a new constitution.

Article 2. The Interim Administration

The Interim Administration of Infaru is tasked with keeping order while there is no constitution or established Infaruan State. It will cease to exist once a constitution is ratified and all of its powers will be orderly transferred according to the new constitution. The Interim Administration is made up of the Infaru staff and holds the power to issue temporary orders that hold the force of law and expire after one week if itâ€™s necessary to ensure order. Any not yet expired orders will immediately expire at the ratification of a new constitution.

Article 3. Ratification of a new constitution

Once the Constituent Assembly has written a new constitution and all of its members have agreed on its ratification this charter will immediately be voided. The new constitution should be democratic and free.

Article 4. The armed forces

The armed forces exist to enforce order during the interim governance of Infaru, they are under the supervision of the Interim Administration. To ensure order is kept they are authorised to use any means necessary. The armed forces will be appropriately integrated into the new Infaruan State with the ratification of the constitution.
